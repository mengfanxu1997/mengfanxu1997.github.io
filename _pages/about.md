Hi! I am Mengfan, and I am a Tenure-Track Assistant Professor in the [Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering](https://www.umass.edu/engineering/about/directory?s=&field_person__profile_type_ref_target_id%5B411%5D=411&field_person__related_programs_target_id%5B91%5D=91&field_person__related_programs_target_id%5B156%5D=156&page=0) and an Adjunct Assistant Professor in the [Manning College of Information & Computer Sciences](https://www.cics.umass.edu) at [UMass Amherst](https://www.umass.edu/). 

I obtained my Ph.D. from the [Department of Industrial Engineering and Management Sciences at Northwestern University](https://www.mccormick.northwestern.edu/industrial/) in 2024, where I was very fortunate and grateful to be advised by [Prof. Diego Klabjan](https://dynresmanagement.com/vita) and to receive invaluable mentorship from the faculty members, a debt of gratitude I can never fully repay. Previously, I obtained my bachelor's degree in Statistics from the [University of Science and Technology of China](https://en.ustc.edu.cn/) in 2018, where I was very appreciative to receive mathematical training and be admitted to a summer exchange program in Biostatistics at the [Yale School of Public Health](https://ysph.yale.edu/) and the [Yale School of Medicine](https://medicine.yale.edu/) in 2017, an experience that opened many doors for me. 

My research group works at the intersection of operations research, machine learning, and artificial intelligence, with a particular emphasis on online learning. We formulate real-time decision-making problems in an imperfect and complex world and develop principled algorithms with provable and generalizable guarantees for these challenging settings. Motivated by operational challenges faced by real-world decision-makers, our work develops new methodologies that capture the intricate structures and complexities underlying these problems, with applications across a broad range of domains. Our research interests include: 

- Online Learning
- Multi-Agent Systems
- Statistical Learning and Algorithm Design
- Stochastic Processes and Simulation Optimization
- Applications in Operations Research
